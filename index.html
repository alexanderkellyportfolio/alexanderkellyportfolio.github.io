SXSW BattleBot IQ

Hardware Engineer, Structural Design & Manufacturing

Overview

About 5 weeks before South by Southwest (SXSW), the UT robotics department was looking for a team of students to build and compete with a 15-pound battlebot. This was an absurdly quick timeline and an exciting challenge to go from nothing to concept to competition.

My Role & Contributions

  • Led structural design and material selection for the entire robot
  • Designed wheel guards, modular forks, and back panel in SolidWorks
  • Milled 6061-T6 aluminum backplate in-house using manual mill
  • Manufactured HDPE wheel guards and components via laser cutting
  • Managed DFM/DFA considerations under tight 5-week timeline
  • Coordinated with team to ensure all components met safety regulations

Results

We placed 2nd in our group and won the robo-rumble match. Despite taking damage, we sustained minimal critical failures and went through many replacement parts throughout the matches. The quick turnaround demonstrated effective design iteration and manufacturing efficiency.

SolidWorks DFM/DFA Material Selection Manual Milling Laser Cutting Assembly

RC Car Drivetrain Project

Design & Fabrication Engineer, Drivetrain and Mounts

Overview

Class project given to create a vehicle from scratch—including the chassis, Ackermann steering system, and rear-wheel-drive drivetrain—resulting in a fully functional car that balanced performance, manufacturability, and reliability.

Technical Details

  • Designed custom pulleys and mounts for the drivetrain using Onshape CAD
  • Implemented multi-stage gear and timing belt reduction system
  • Performed FEA analysis in ANSYS to validate structural integrity
  • Applied tolerance analysis and GD&T principles throughout design
  • Iterated through multiple prototypes using 3D printing and laser cutting
  • Developed Ackermann steering geometry for optimal turning radius

Design Evolution

Our initial drivetrain design was a spur gear system, but due to backlash between teeth, they deteriorated beyond reliable use. We switched to a timing belt system to avoid backlash and absorb more vibrations, making the drivetrain more durable and reliable through rapid iteration.

Onshape ANSYS FEA Mechanism Design Tolerance Analysis DFA/DFM 3D Printing Laser Cutting Soldering

CNC Ball in Cube

CNC Machining Project

Overview

Started with a 3" cube of 6061 aluminum and ran 4 operations per each face (roughing, spiral finish, edge finish, and a chamfer) to create a precision ball-in-cube sculpture demonstrating advanced CNC programming and multi-setup machining.

Manufacturing Process

  • Programmed all toolpaths in Fusion360 CAM software
  • Executed roughing operations to remove bulk material
  • Applied spiral finishing for smooth internal surfaces
  • Performed edge finishing and chamfering for clean aesthetics
  • Managed workholding across multiple setups (6 faces)
  • Maintained tight tolerances throughout all operations

Technical Challenges

The project required careful planning of tool paths to avoid collisions, strategic workholding to access all faces while maintaining part integrity, and precise CAM programming to achieve the final geometry. Each face required careful coordination to ensure the internal ball remained free-floating without excessive clearance.

Skills & Tools

Fusion360 CAM 3-Axis CNC Workholding Toolpath Planning GD&T
